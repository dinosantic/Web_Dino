Street

photography

Street photography also sometimes called candid photography is a genre of photography that records everyday life in a public place. Street photography is mostly focused on people and their behavior in public and everyday life. Street photographers do not necessarily have a social purpose in mind, but they prefer to isolate and capture moments which might otherwise go unnoticed. Framing and timing can be key aspects of the craft with the aim of some street photography being to create images at a decisive or poignant moment.

Landscape

photography

Landscape photography shows spaces within the world, sometimes vast and unending, but other times microscopic. Landscape photographs typically capture the presence of nature but can also focus on man-made features or disturbances of landscapes. Landscape photographers are capturing an image that embodies the spirit of the outdoors. It carries a sense of being there to see something incredible. When viewers look at your work, you want them to feel the same emotions that you felt, standing in the middle of nature and bringing back something amazing.

Nature

photography

Nature photography is a wide range of photography taken outdoors and devoted to displaying natural elements such as wildlife, plants and close-ups of natural scenes and textures. Nature photography tends to put a stronger emphasis on the aesthetic value of the photo than other photography genres. "Nature photography" overlaps the fields of, and is sometimes considered an overarching category including, 'wildlife photography', 'landscape photography' and "garden photography".

Portrait

photography

Portrait photography is a photograph of a person or group of people that captures the personality of the subject by using effective lighting, backdrops, and poses. A portrait picture might be artistic, or it might be clinical, as part of a medical study. A photographer's goal is to take a carefully decided photograph of a person's distinguishing facial features, while capturing the person's attitude, identity, and personality. The photo may include a blurred background and the person's body, but those factors are not emphasized in the image.
